NeQIS: Neural Quadratic Implicit Surfaces

Abstract

We introduce NeQIS, a novel neural surface reconstruction method that accurately recovers 3D surfaces through image-based neural rendering. We observe and theoretically demonstrate that current MLP-based neural representations of the SDFs, which rely solely on linear layers with ReLU activation functions, are, in fact, a piecewise linear approximation of the SDF. As a result, they struggle to capture higher-order surface details such as curvature. NeQIS addresses this theoretical limitation by introducing quadratic layers, i.e. layers where each neuron performs a quadratic transform of its input. We theoretically demonstrate that such quadratic layers provide a better approximation of the SDF as they enable us to directly model the second-order term in the Taylor expansion of the SDF. Our extensive experiments on benchmark datasets demonstrate that NeQIS outperforms the state-of-the-art in high-quality surface reconstruction, especially for objects and scenes with complex and thin structures.
